Column Name Meanings

  • Clump Thickness: Measures how densely packed cells are in a tissue sample. Low values (closer to 1) mean cells are scattered, while high values (closer to 10) indicate dense clusters or clumps.
  • Uniformity of Cell Size: Measures the consistency of cell sizes in the sample. Cancer cells tend to vary in size and shape, so higher values suggest greater variation.
  • Uniformity of Cell Shape: Measures the consistency of cell shapes in the sample. Similar to Uniformity of Cell Size, but focusing on cell shapes.
  • Marginal Adhesion: Indicates how well cells stick together. Lower values may suggest cancerous cells losing adhesion ability.
  • Single Epithelial Cell Size: Related to the uniformity of cell size and shape. Enlarged epithelial cells may indicate malignancy.
  • Bare Nuclei: Refers to nuclei not surrounded by cytoplasm. Higher values suggest the presence of these nuclei, often seen in benign tumors.
  • Bland Chromatin: Describes the texture of the nucleus. Benign cells typically have uniform chromatin, while cancer cells may have coarse and clumped chromatin.
  • Normal Nucleoli: Measures the size and prominence of nucleoli, small structures in the nucleus. Cancer cells often have larger and more prominent nucleoli.
  • Mitoses: Indicates the rate of cell division. Cancer cells divide rapidly and uncontrollably, so higher values suggest a higher likelihood of malignancy.
